## Repository overview

This repository groups five Sahelian and interior West African language channels into one structured access layer:

- Bambara
- Moore
- Dagbani
- Zarma
- Dendi

Its purpose is clear: give this regionally coherent and historically Islamic cluster a focused and practical place for discovery, integration, and multilingual Islamic content workflows.

---

## Why this repository exists

This cluster is built on real geographic and historical proximity.

Bambara, Moore, Dagbani, Zarma, and Dendi are all languages of the Sahel belt and interior West Africa — a region where Islam has been the dominant religious and cultural force for centuries, carried by trade networks, scholarly traditions, and community institutions that predate the colonial era.

This repository creates a cleaner layer for:

- regional discoverability
- structured Islamic content access
- multilingual publishing workflows
- localization and deployment
- educational product building
- cleaner repository segmentation

---

## Why this cluster works

Bambara anchors Mali — a country with one of the deepest Islamic scholarly traditions in West Africa, home to the historic centers of Timbuktu and Djenné. Moore anchors Burkina Faso's majority Muslim population. Dagbani covers the predominantly Muslim northern regions of Ghana. Zarma and Dendi cover Niger and parts of Benin — societies with Islam at the center of daily life and community structure.

This is a geographically contiguous and historically grounded cluster — one of the most coherent in this repository series.

## Regional value

This repository improves more than access.

It improves regional coherence, historical relevance, and deployment clarity.

Instead of dispersing these Sahelian languages inside a broad West African or multilingual layer, this repository gives them a more precise identity rooted in shared geography, shared Islamic heritage, and shared content access needs — and a more useful implementation surface for communities that have been underserved by Islamic digital infrastructure despite their deep connection to the faith.
